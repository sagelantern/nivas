# Stay Nivas — GM Relationship Strategy

This document is internal. It outlines how Stay Nivas builds and maintains relationships with general managers and hospitality professionals.

---

## The Goal

GMs should see Stay Nivas as a credible, thoughtful voice they *want* reviewing their property. Not someone to manage or comp as a favor — someone whose opinion they'd genuinely value.

---

## How GMs Find Us

1. **The review itself.** A well-written, specific review that shows genuine understanding of what a property is trying to do is the single best calling card. GMs Google their property obsessively. A Stay Nivas review should rank and impress.

2. **Direct outreach (post-stay).** After publishing a review, a brief email to the GM linking the piece. Not asking for anything — just sharing. The tone: "I stayed at your property and wrote about the experience. Thought you might find it interesting."

3. **Pre-stay introduction.** For properties we're planning to visit, a short email introducing Stay Nivas, linking past reviews, and mentioning the upcoming stay. Not requesting upgrades — just making ourselves known. What happens after that is up to them.

---

## What Makes a GM Pay Attention

Based on what hospitality professionals actually value:

- **Specificity.** Mentioning a staff member by name. Noticing a design detail that took months to get right. Commenting on the breakfast program's philosophy, not just the eggs.

- **Understanding intent.** The best compliment you can give a GM is showing you understood what they were *trying* to create — even if the execution wasn't perfect.

- **Fair criticism.** A review that's all praise is useless. A review that notes what didn't work — with the nuance of someone who gets how hard hospitality is — builds credibility.

- **Beautiful writing.** GMs share well-written reviews with their teams, their ownership groups, and their peers. A review worth quoting is a review worth remembering.

- **No transactional energy.** The moment a GM feels like the review is leverage for future comps, the relationship is dead. We write because we care about hospitality. Upgrades and access come as a byproduct of credibility, never as a goal stated aloud.

## The Media Kit (Future)

Once we have 8–10 published reviews, create a one-page media kit (PDF):

- **Who:** One paragraph on Yash + the Stay Nivas lens
- **What:** Hospitality journal, long-form reviews, [X] monthly readers
- **Past Properties:** Logos of reviewed properties
- **Sample Review:** Link to the strongest piece
- **Contact:** yash@staynivas.com

Keep it to one page. GMs don't read decks.

---

## Properties to Prioritize

Focus early reviews on properties where:

1. The GM is accessible and engaged (boutique properties, not mega-chains)
2. The property has a genuine point of view (design, philosophy, location)
3. You have a real opinion — positive or nuanced. Don't review forgettable stays.
4. The brand is one where GM relationships compound (Alila, Andaz, Soho House, Aman, EDITION, Ace, Nobu, 1 Hotels)

---

## Relationship Cadence

- **After review:** One email, as above. No follow-up if no response.
- **Return visit:** Brief note letting them know you're coming back. This signals the review was the beginning of a relationship, not a transaction.
- **Occasional sharing:** If you see their property in the news or they win an award, a quick congratulatory note. GMs remember who pays attention.
- **Never ask for comps directly.** Ever. If they offer, accept graciously. If they don't, pay full rate and write the review anyway. That's what builds credibility.
